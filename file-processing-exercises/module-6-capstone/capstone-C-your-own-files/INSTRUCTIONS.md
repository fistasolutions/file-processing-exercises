# Capstone C -- Your Own Files

**Build** -- Apply the complete file processing workflow to your actual Downloads or Desktop folder

## Goal

Apply all seven workflow steps to your ACTUAL Downloads folder, Desktop, or another folder you have been meaning to organize. Real files. Real stakes. Real learning. The goal is not just a clean folder -- it is a documented SYSTEM for keeping it clean.

## What You Have

- Your own files. No starter files are provided for this capstone.

## Important: Safety First

**START WITH BACKUP.** This is not negotiable. You are working with your real files. Back up your entire target folder before touching anything.

## Your Tasks

### Step 1: Choose Your Target

Pick a folder: Downloads, Desktop, Documents, or any folder with accumulated files.

### Step 2: Survey

Create a complete inventory. How many files? What types? How big? What is the oldest file? Are there duplicates?

### Step 3: Backup

Create a complete, verified backup. Do NOT proceed until you have confirmed the backup matches the original.

### Step 4: Plan

Design your organization rules and naming conventions. Write them in a file.

### Step 5: Test

Apply your rules to 3-5 files across different categories. Verify the results make sense.

### Step 6: Execute

Organize your entire folder according to your rules.

### Step 7: Verify

Confirm all files are accounted for. No files lost, no files misplaced.

### Step 8: Document

Create `MY-FILE-TOOLKIT.md` containing:

- Your folder structure and why you chose it
- Your naming conventions
- Your organization rules
- Your backup strategy going forward
- Quick search tips for finding common files
- A monthly maintenance checklist

## Expected Results

- A clean, organized folder
- `MY-FILE-TOOLKIT.md` -- your personal file management system
- A backup of the original state (keep this for at least a week in case you need to undo anything)

## Time Estimate

2-4 hours, depending on how many files you have and how messy they are.

## Reflection

1. What surprised you about your own files? Were there files you forgot about, or files you thought you had deleted?
2. Which workflows from the course were most useful for YOUR files? Which did you skip or modify?
3. Will you actually maintain this system? What is the minimum maintenance that would keep it organized?
